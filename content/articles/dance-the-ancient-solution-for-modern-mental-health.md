## Dance is Ingrained in Our DNA

There's a reason babies know how to bop around to the music as early as 5 months old — it's deeply ingrained in our DNA. Dancing has been an integral part of the human experience for millennia. Our ancestors used dancing as a way to bond and communicate with each other. They danced to reach enlightened experiences. They danced to get ready for battle. They danced to celebrate life and to celebrate death.

Today through research we can prove what our ancestors have known for millennia.

Dancing is now scientifically proven to:

- Improve memory and greatly reduce the likelihood of dementia and Alzheimer's
- Strengthen bones
- Improve coordination, muscle tone, and balance
- Increase confidence and compassion
- Reduce stress, which can lead to many chronic diseases

Participants in a UCLA study that utilized free-form dance said things like: "I have a greater sense of meaning," "I feel more present in my body," "I have a greater sense of purpose," and "I'm more relaxed."

## Good for the Heart, and for the Soul

We all know dancing gets our heart rates up — but that doesn't begin to touch the synergistic effect that dancing has on health. Regular western healthcare views folks' physical conditions separately from mental conditions. But advancements in psychosomatic research tell us that body and mind are basically one thing. They can only be understood as an interdependent system.

A study published in the *American Journal of Preventive Medicine* in 2016 found that participants over age 40 who danced at moderate intensity were **46% less likely to die from cardiovascular disease** than people who did not dance — within a 10-year time span. The same study found that people who walked for exercise at the same moderate intensity were only 25% less likely to die from cardiovascular disease.

Dancing proved to be almost **twice as effective** at preventing heart disease as walking — at the same intensity level. The reason appears to lie in dancing's extraordinary variety of movement — the twisting, pinching, shaking, gliding, reaching, and stretching our body does when we dance, compared to the controlled forward fall of walking.

## The Mental Effects Are Undeniable

A study from UCLA explored the mental effects of free-form conscious dancing on participants with anxiety, depression, chronic pain, a history of trauma, and a history of addiction. All of these conditions saw vast improvements in over 80% of people.

**96% of people suffering from depression and anxiety said dancing effectively helped them cope.**

That's a massive success rate. For comparison, the efficacy rates for depression medications are about 30-40% depending on the study and drug.

Here's an experiment: Stand up, turn on your favorite dance songs, and move your body freely for 4 minutes. Can you do this without feeling an instant improvement in your mood? Pretty hard not to feel happy when you're dancing.

## Lose Weight and Process Trauma… at the Same Time

One of the most exciting aspects of free-form dance is how effectively it helps people process trauma. Janet Lo, a UCLA grad who teaches a healing-focused style of dance, explains: "Trauma, anxiety, and depression can cause people to dissociate and disconnect from their bodies, but conscious dance allows them to get in touch with all their senses."

Trauma is stored in the body and can manifest as chronic pain. Dance helps to process and integrate these emotions into our current reality so they are no longer "stuck" in us. Dance puts us in the present moment, puts us in touch with our bodies and emotions, and allows us to explore our own feelings and memories in a self-guided safe way.

Traumatic flashbacks can cause the Broca's area of the brain (responsible for language) to go offline, making verbal communication difficult. Psychologists who went to Rwanda to help people recover from genocidal trauma found that talk therapy didn't help — the Rwandan way of healing was dancing in community to process grief. Cultures from all over the world have used dance to process emotions as a community, and still do to this day.

On average, dancing burns 300-400 calories per hour, and when coupled with a healthy diet, can result in losing 1-3 pounds per week.

## Make It a Habit

Make it a habit. Make it your lifestyle. Make it a part of your family's culture. If you want to make tomorrow better than today, teach your children and your children's children to dance.

So it turns out "Shake your groove thing" is some damn good advice. It's been ingrained in our DNA since the beginning of time to move our bodies in an expressive and fun way. Let's reclaim it.
