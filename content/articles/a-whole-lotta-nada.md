**Feeling strung out? Addicted? Try needles in your ears!?**

## The Need for a Different Way

The year is 1974 in the South Bronx and Dr. Michael Smith treats addicts. He has seen a lot of addicts come and go at Lincoln Hospital. Some make it out alive. Some don't. Methadone treatments help some, but this is still an addictive substance that causes liver, nerve, and brain damage when used long-term.

Doctor Smith wanted to find another treatment that helped patients with addiction recovery without doing more damage or causing another addiction. Only 2 years before, a neurosurgeon living in Hong Kong — H.L. Wen, M.D. — discovered that acupuncture could be used very effectively for treating withdrawal symptoms during addiction recovery. Though acupuncture has been used for centuries to alleviate pain and treat disease, this was the first time the ancient healing modality was used to treat addiction.

Dr. Smith adopted this modality and with help from local acupuncturists, formed a protocol first given in 1974. The initial treatment placed one needle in the lung point on the ear and used electrostimulation. Why the ear? Ease of treatment, the ability to treat multiple patients at once, and way less invasive than a full body session. For these reasons, even the military has adopted aural acupuncture.

## High Rates of Success Take the Treatment Worldwide

Acupuncture itself has been around for about 3,000 years and is still thriving today *because it works.* An acupuncture "map" shows the human body with meridians — pathways through which our vital life force energy (Qi) flows. By placing needles in specific points along these meridians, Qi is allowed to flow freely, which maintains health in the body.

Dr. Smith's aural acupuncture protocol for addiction recovery was so incredibly effective that by 1975 it became a permanent part of the Lincoln Detox program. Staff discovered that manual stimulation of the needle was even more effective than electrostimulation, making the treatment even cheaper and more accessible.

The outer ear is like an energy switchboard that sends electrical signals to the brain. Placing needles in specific points stimulates the release of endorphins while lowering stress hormones and promoting relaxation.

With much experimentation, a more effective protocol was created that includes five ear points. **This is the NADA protocol we still use today:**

- **Sympathetic** — helps with overall relaxation by calming the nervous system
- **Shen Men / Spirit Gate** — reduces nervous anxiety
- **Kidney Point** — heals internal organs while calming fear
- **Liver Point** — purifies the blood, calms aggressive tendencies, and aids in detoxification
- **Lung Point** — helps the patient let go of grief

During the crack cocaine epidemic of the mid-80s, the protocol proved just as effective in treating the cravings, dysphasia, and anxiety associated with crack withdrawals. In 1985, the Lincoln detox staff along with other chemical dependency professionals formed the group NADA — the National Acupuncture Detoxification Association. Thousands of practitioners worldwide now use this protocol with life-changing results.

## Studies Show…

By 1992, the Lincoln Detox Clinic had successfully helped 15,000 crack patients get sober within a 5-year time span — proving it to be vastly more effective than anything else available at the time. 90% of pregnant women enrolled in the detox program were drug-free by the time they gave birth.

The NADA protocol has been proven to decrease cravings for all kinds of addictive substances including cigarettes, alcohol, opioids, heroin, crack, meth, and even gambling and food addictions. Participants and healthcare providers report reduced anxiety, fewer sleep disturbances, and a reduced need for pharmaceuticals.

## Not Just for Addicts — All Trauma

NADA is not just for addicts. A unique characteristic of the NADA protocol is its power and utility in a group setting. Acupuncturists Without Borders, a disaster relief organization, uses NADA in all of their relief work. They now have over 40 established care centers in 16+ countries, providing relief for traumatized refugees, migrants, and natural disaster survivors worldwide.

In a NADA session, participants sit together in a group for 30-60 minutes after needles are placed in their ears. At about the 25-minute mark, something amazing happens — every group session is unique, but nearly every time, you'll see everyone in the group shift. Even talkative groups become more still and quiet and reflective.

Trauma shared is trauma reduced. There is a sense of solidarity in knowing that you are not going through this alone.

## Courage, Perseverance, and Support

NADA is kind of like a superpower that makes your chances of recovery a lot higher by making the process more bearable. Recovering from addiction — whether it's smoking tobacco, social media, or heroin — takes courage, strength, and lots of community support. Incorporating NADA into your recovery will definitely increase your chances of success.
