Chinese Medicine has a long and storied history of use in the treatment of metabolic disorders. With its roots tracing back centuries, Chinese Medicine is an ancient form of medicine that utilizes herbology and other modalities to treat a wide range of ailments.

In particular, Chinese Medicine has been used generation after generation to address various metabolic disorders, including diabetes, obesity, high cholesterol, high blood pressure, IBS and Crohn's disease. By utilizing natural herbs as well as nutritional therapy and acupuncture treatments, Chinese Medicine practitioners have helped countless individuals manage metabolic conditions effectively with minimal to no side effects.

**The efficacy of this system speaks for itself:** *Today millions around the world successfully rely on Chinese Medicine to help them maintain their health and wellness.*

## What is a Metabolic Disorder?

Metabolic disorder is an umbrella term for a large group of related health conditions — specifically, health issues related to the body's ability to produce, process, and store energy, including diabetes, obesity, and hyperlipidemia (high cholesterol).

The five most common metabolic disorders are:

1. **Diabetes** — the most common form of metabolic disorder.
2. **Hemochromatosis** — a genetic disorder in which too much iron accumulates in the body, leading to organ damage.
3. **Gaucher's disease** — an inherited disorder that affects the metabolism of certain fats in the body.
4. **Nutrition and metabolism disorders** — problems with how the body processes food for energy. Many common digestive issues such as Crohn's and IBS fall under this umbrella.
5. **Metabolic syndrome** — a combination of risk factors that increase a person's risk for heart disease, stroke, and diabetes.

## The Use of Chinese Medicine in Treating Metabolic Disorders

Herbal formulas are one of the most commonly used components of Chinese Medicine in treating metabolic disorders. Herbs have been utilized for centuries to address a wide range of ailments. In particular, these herbal remedies can be incredibly effective when it comes to managing various metabolic disorders such as diabetes, obesity, hyperlipidemia, hypertension, and more.

By utilizing natural herbs alongside other modalities such as nutritional therapy and acupuncture treatments, practitioners help individuals manage these conditions effectively with minimal side effects.

## How Chinese Medicine Reduces Symptoms

Whole herb extracts used in Chinese herbology make use of each herb's unique phytokine profile to treat all manner of diseases.

Phytokines, or plant-derived bioactive compounds, are gaining increasing attention for their potential role in treating metabolic disorders. A variety of phytokines found in plants and herbs have been studied and found to possess beneficial effects in managing diabetes, obesity, and other metabolic disorders. These compounds have been shown to act as powerful modulators of metabolic functions — helping the body to heal itself.

## 5 Benefits of Chinese Medicine Over Pharmacology

- **Natural herbs are less likely to cause side effects than pharmaceuticals** — especially when taken long term.
- **Herbal remedies can be tailored to each individual's unique needs**, health goals, and lifestyle — providing truly personalized care.
- **Traditional Chinese Medicine takes a holistic approach**, addressing the underlying causes of metabolic disorders rather than just treating symptoms.
- **Acupuncture treatments have been shown to reduce BMI** and improve overall wellness in addition to managing metabolic disorders directly.
- **Lower systemic risk** — unlike pharmaceutical interventions, herbal protocols rarely cause dependency or cascading side effects.

## Examples of Successful Treatments

One study found that two Chinese herbs, Coptis chinensis (huang lian) and Scutellaria baicalensis (huang qin), were effective in reducing fasting blood glucose levels in patients with type 2 diabetes.

Another study found that acupuncture by itself was effective in decreasing the body mass index (BMI) of patients — no herbs required.
